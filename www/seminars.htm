Past seminars

Algebraic Graphs by Andrey Mokhov 15 November 2017, 13:30-14:30 @ USB.3.032

We will discuss a new way of thinking about graphs, where instead of focusing on details (vertices and edges) we take a step back and discover a simple and unusual algebraic structure that characterises graphs, much like the ring theory characterises numbers. Algebraic graphs are implemented in a Haskell library.

Structuring Effectful Computations by Georgy Lukyanov 1 November 2017, 16:00-17:00 @ USB.3.032

Type systems of modern programming languages provide means to statically control side effects, such as mutable state, exceptions, IO, and many more. One of the most famous ways to do so are Haskell's Monads. During this seminar we're going to look at a couple of cases where controlled side effects make our lives easier.

The Power of Randomness by Andrey Mokhov 30 October 2017, 16:00-17:00 @ Room E303, Merz Court

Randomness can be a powerful ally and in this seminar we will hopefully get to know it a little better. We will discuss several examples where randomness allows us to find simple and effective solutions to problems that are otherwise difficult to tackle.
